I am a second-year at The University of Texas at Austin, majoring in Electrical and Computer Engineering and Plan II Honors.

I'm an undergraduate researcher in the Quantum Circuits Group, led by Professor Shyam Shankar of the ECE department at UT. The research group "builds superconducting and semiconducting quantum devices and circuits for applications in quantum information science and engineering," and this work is at the intersection of nanofabrication, superconductivity, and microwave engineering, among other fields. I am currently working on some power electronics projects, including designing a custom power supply PCB (with many types of voltage regulators on-board) for our microwave electronics setup and building an affordable programmable power supply that is highly accurate and precise using consumer electronic components.

This summer, I will also be working at Intel in The Numerical Hardware Group, a new applied research group within Intel's graphics group that focuses on computer arithmetic and mathematical hardware.

Last fall, I took ECE courses on quantum mechanics, lasers, and linear systems and signals, part one of Plan II's philosophy sequence, an interdisciplinary course on quantum information science, and classical mechanics. I was also a violinist in the UT University Orchestra!

This spring, I took introductory solid-state physics, electromagnetic engineering, part one of the undergraduate algebraic structures sequence, part two of the quantum information science course from last semester, a course on applied linear algebra, and part two of Plan II's philosophy sequence. I also continued playing the violin in the UT University Orchestra!
